# Government officials

## Active lawyers

### Brett Shumate

[Brett Shumate](https://www.linkedin.com/in/brett-shumate-54b51935/) is the Principal Deputy Assistant Attorney General, Civil Division. He is a political appointee.

He argued for Trump in Washington v Trump. The judge, a Reagan appointee, had this to say about him: "Frankly I have difficulty understanding how a member of the Bar could state unequivocally that this is a constitutional order."

### Drew C. Ensign

[Drew Ensign](https://www.linkedin.com/in/drew-ensign-43383b11/) is the Deputy Assistant Attorney General, a political appointee.

In the [Abrego Garcia](/cases/abrego-garcia) case, he told [Judge Xinis](https://bsky.app/profile/annabower.bsky.social/post/3lmkj3raobs2j) that he had no information about what had been done to comply with her order, 8 days after the order had been entered.

## Fired lawyers

### Erez Reuveni

[Erez Reuveni](https://www.linkedin.com/in/erez-reuveni-5b796b18/) (15 years at DOJ) represented the US government in [Abrego Garcia v Noem](https://www.courtlistener.com/docket/69777799/abrego-garcia-v-noem/?filed_after=&filed_before=&entry_gte=&entry_lte=&order_by=desc).

On April 5, 2025 the [New York Times reported](https://www.nytimes.com/live/2025/04/05/us/trump-news-tariffs) that he was put on leave for failing to "follow a directive from [his] superiors".

On April 15, 2025, the [Wall Street Journal](https://www.wsj.com/us-news/law/this-lawyer-defended-republicans-and-democrats-his-candor-cost-him-his-job-b3515a38) reported that he had since been fired.


## Resigned lawyers

### Danielle Sassoon

Danielle Sassoon (8 years at DOJ), co-chief of Criminal Appeals for SDNY, resigned rather than go along with the government's scheme in [US v Adams](/cases/adams).

### Denise Cheung

Denise Cheung (24 years at DOJ) was the head of the criminal division in the DC office. [Politico reports](https://www.politico.com/news/2025/02/18/denise-cheung-us-attorneys-office-washington-020363) that she resigned rather than go along with an administration scheme to open a criminal investigation into recipients of a Biden-era climate change fund.

### Hagan Scotten

[Hagen Scotten](https://www.linkedin.com/in/hagan-scotten-868352354/) (10 years at DOJ), Chief of Appeals for SDNY, resigned rather than go along with the government's scheme in [US v. Adams](/cases/adams).

### Jenn Clarke

[Jenn Clarke](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jennifer-clarke-299180352/) (9 years at DOJ), Deputy Chief in the Public Integrity Section, resigned rather than go along with the government's scheme in [US v Adams](/cases/adams).

### John Keller

[John Keller](https://www.linkedin.com/in/john-keller-41b424319/) (14 years at DOJ), acting Chief of the Public Integrity Section, resigned rather than go along with the government's scheme in [US v Adams](/cases/adams).

### Kevin Driscoll

Kevin Driscoll resigned rather than go along with the government's scheme in [US v Adams](/cases/adams).

### Marco Palmieri

[Marco Palmieri](https://www.linkedin.com/in/marco-a-palmieri-91b7ba335/) (12 years at DOJ), Deputy Chief in the Criminal Division, resigned rather than go along with the government's scheme in [US v Adams](/cases/adams).

### Olivia Horton

[Olivia Horton](https://www.linkedin.com/in/olivia-horton-a4b858141/) (6 months at DOJ) represented the US government in [Doe v OPM](https://www.courtlistener.com/docket/69582338/doe-v-office-of-personnel-management/).

Per [a court filing in that case](https://storage.courtlistener.com/recap/gov.uscourts.dcd.276820/gov.uscourts.dcd.276820.34.0.pdf), she resigned from the Department of Justice on March 7, 2025.

Per [an April 6, 2025 court filing in the Abrego Garcia case](https://storage.courtlistener.com/recap/gov.uscourts.ca4.178258/gov.uscourts.ca4.178258.8.1.pdf), she appears to now be working for Quinn Emanuel.

### Rob Heberle

[Rob Heberle](https://www.linkedin.com/in/robert-heberle-75b5bb82/) (13 years at DOJ), Director in the Public Integrity Section, resigned rather than go along with the government's scheme in [US v Adams](/cases/adams).


### Sean Murphy

[Sean Murphy](https://www.linkedin.com/in/seanmurphyjd/) (6 years at DOJ) was an AUSA.

Per [an NPR report](https://www.npr.org/2025/03/20/nx-s1-5330879/trump-justice-doj-prosecutor-resignation-jan-6), he resigned in protest of the direction the DOJ was going.

Robert Cerna is a career official for ICE.

In the [JGG v Trump](/cases/jgg-v-trump) case, he filed a declaration stating that absence of evidence is evidence of terrorist risk:

<div>
The lack of a criminal record does not indicate they pose a limited threat. [...] It demonstrates that they are terrorists with regard to whom we lack a complete profile.
</div>

</blockquote>

### Michael G Kozak

Michael Kozak is a 79-year-old career employee of the State Department. He was trotted out to [file a declaration](https://storage.courtlistener.com/recap/gov.uscourts.mdd.578815/gov.uscourts.mdd.578815.63.0_1.pdf) in the [Abrego Garcia case](/cases/abrego-garcia).
